---
title: Artistic Research
---

Qin Wenbao's artistic research explores the integration of abstract art with contemporary theatrical costume. Working from the idea of performance costume as an abstract canvas, his practice treats costume as a medium through which visual art, movement, material, and theatrical space can be translated into embodied form.

His research focuses on two connected directions: the dynamic manifestation of abstract art within theatrical space, and the transformation of Eastern ink wash aesthetics into perceptible theatrical language. Through this approach, costume becomes a site of corporeal translation, where calligraphic gesture, material structure, and the performing body meet.

As a theatre designer and educator at the National Academy of Chinese Theatre Arts, Qin Wenbao links design practice with teaching, international exchange, and academic presentation. His work has been presented through World Stage Design, Prague Quadrennial, Critical Costume, World Costume in Action, USITT, and other international platforms for scenography, costume design, and theatre education.
